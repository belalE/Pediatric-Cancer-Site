UCSC Treehouse Public Data

About UCSC Treehouse Public Data

The Treehouse Childhood Cancer Initiative shares pediatric cancer genomic data using tools developed by UCSC Genomics Institute. Users can conduct a cross-comparison gene expression analysis against both children and adult tumors. The UCSC Treehouse Childhood Cancer Initiative strives to identify situations where approved drugs may work on a child suffering from cancer. They have collected various RNA gene expression data that is also made available for users to download and visualize.

Visit the Website

TumorMap

The UCSC TumorMap displays interactive samples in the Treehouse dataset positioned according to their RNA profiles. Users also can color the samples based on dataset features. This feature is best used to separate smaller groups of samples.

Cluster Browser

The Cluster Browser displays interactive samples in the Treehouse dataset positioned according to their RNA profiles. Users also can color the samples based on dataset features. This feature is best used to show relationships among larger groupd of samples.

Xena

UCSC Xena allows users to find correlations and relationships among samples in the Treehouse dataset. Users may add, remove, or rearrange pieces of data in the tool, such as genes and other features.
